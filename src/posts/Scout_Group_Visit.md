We recently had the pleasure of hosting our first outreach activity with the 7th Warrington West Scout Group. It was a fantastic evening dedicated to the world of aerospace, where we shared our passion for rocketry with a group of incredibly engaged and curious Scouts.

## Model Rocketry 101

The session kicked off with a crash course in "Model Rocketry 101." We walked the Scouts through the 5 stages of flight:

1. **Ignition**: Starting the combustion with a current through the igniter.
2. **Thrust**: Accelerating skyward as the propellant burns.
3. **Coast**: Reaching apogee (the highest point) once the propellant is exhausted.
4. **Ejection**: The critical moment when the delay grain finishes burning and the parachute is pushed out.
5. **Descent**: A safe float back to Earth, ready for the next flight.

## Show and Tell: Real Rocket Hardware

The highlight of the evening was undoubtedly the "show and tell" session. It's one thing to see a diagram on a screen, but it's another thing entirely to hold the parts in your hands! We brought along Banana Split, our award-winning rocket from the 2022-23 season (which took home the Best Airframe Award), to give the Scouts a look at a complete build.

We also passed around:

- **Aerodynamics**: Real nosecones and various fin designs to explain how we maintain stability.
- **Avionics**: The brains of the operation, including our last years flight computer "LIFTS" and a yagi-uda antenna.
- **The Motor**: We explained the internal anatomy of the motor, showing how the delay grain and ejection charge work together.

## Fun Facts & Future Careers

We wrapped up the technical side with some "Fun Facts", We shared that our upcoming rocket, the PL-26, is aiming for an altitude of 580m and a top speed of 268 mph. For our dedicated Mach competition entry next year, we even look at breaking the sound barrier at Mach 1.3.

We also spoke about how these skills translate to the real world. From Astronauts to Propulsion Engineers, we highlighted the local opportunities in the UK space sector with organizations like Skyrora, ESA, and the UK Space Agency.

## A Huge Thank You

We want to extend a massive thank you to the 7th Warrington West Scout Group for having us. It was lovely to see the Scouts so engaged, asking brilliant questions and demonstrating a real knack for engineering logic during our quiz.

We had a wonderful time sharing our journey so far and hope we've inspired a few future rocket scientists!
